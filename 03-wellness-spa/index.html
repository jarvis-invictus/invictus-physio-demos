Conditions We Specialize In

Every child's journey is unique. We bring specialized expertise to a wide range of pediatric conditions, always with compassion at the center.

🧠

Cerebral Palsy

Tailored motor therapy to improve movement, posture, and daily independence. We celebrate every small victory with your child.

🌱

Developmental Delay

When milestones take a little longer, we're here to gently guide your child forward with evidence-based interventions.

🧩

Autism — Motor Skills

Focused sensory-motor therapy that helps children on the spectrum build coordination, body awareness, and confidence.

🦒

Torticollis

Gentle stretching and positioning techniques to help your baby achieve full range of neck movement, naturally and comfortably.

👣

Flat Foot

Strengthening exercises and gait training to develop proper arch support and confident, balanced walking patterns.

🏥

Post-Surgery Rehab

Compassionate recovery programs that restore strength and mobility after orthopedic or neurological surgeries.

🎨 Play = Therapy = Progress

Therapy That Feels Like Play ✨

Children don't sit still for exercises — and we don't expect them to. Our sessions are designed around what your child loves: colors, games, songs, and imagination. When a child is having fun, their brain is learning fastest.

🎮

Game-Based Exercises

Each therapeutic exercise is wrapped in a game. Your child thinks they're playing — they're actually building strength and coordination.

👨‍👩‍👧

Parents Are Partners

You're not just watching — you're part of the team. We teach you techniques to continue therapy at home, making progress a family journey.

📊

Progress You Can See

Monthly milestone reports with photos and videos. We document every achievement, no matter how small, because every step matters.

Meet Dr. Priya Sawant

BPTh | Certified in Pediatric Neurodevelopmental Therapy

Dr. Priya didn't just choose pediatric physiotherapy — it chose her. After witnessing a child take their first steps during her internship, she knew this was her calling. Over 12 years and 500+ families later, her gentle hands and warm smile remain the constant that parents and children trust.

Questions Parents Ask Us 🤗

We understand your concerns. Here are honest answers to the questions every parent has when considering pediatric physiotherapy.

The earlier, the better — but it's never too late. We see infants as young as 2-3 months for conditions like torticollis, and children up to 14 years for motor skill development. If your pediatrician has flagged a concern, or if you notice your child isn't hitting milestones on the expected timeline, don't wait. Early intervention gives the brain the best chance to create new movement pathways. A free developmental screening at our clinic can help you understand where your child stands.

Every child's journey is different — we don't believe in one-size-fits-all treatment plans. After the initial assessment, Dr. Priya will create a personalized plan. Some children show remarkable progress in 8-12 sessions, while others with complex conditions benefit from ongoing support over months. We typically start with 2 sessions per week and adjust as your child progresses. You'll receive monthly progress reports so you can see exactly how far your little one has come.

Bring your child in comfortable, loose clothing that allows free movement. If your child has a favorite toy or comfort object, bring that too — it helps them feel safe in a new environment. Also bring any medical reports, developmental assessments, or doctor's referral letters you have. Most importantly, bring your questions and concerns. We want the first session to be a conversation, not just an examination. And don't forget a water bottle and a small snack — happy tummies make for better sessions!

Yes! This is the question we love answering. Our clinic is designed to feel like a playroom, not a hospital. We use colorful mats, balls, slides, toys, music, and bubble machines. Dr. Priya has a gift for making children feel like they're playing rather than doing "exercises." Most of our little patients smile through their sessions, and many actually look forward to coming back. Some even cry when it's time to leave! We believe that a child who feels safe and happy learns faster — and the results prove it.
