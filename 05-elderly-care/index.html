Conditions We Treat

Every Brain Injury is Unique.
So is Our Treatment.

From the first days after a stroke to long-term Parkinson's management — we treat the full spectrum of neurological conditions with evidence-based, compassionate care.

🧠

Post-Stroke Rehabilitation

Regain movement, speech coordination, and daily independence. Our stroke program has helped hundreds of Pune families — from Sahyadri Hospital referrals to patients from Deccan, Sinhagad Road, and beyond. Early intervention makes all the difference.

🤲

Parkinson's Disease Therapy

LSVT BIG certified exercises to improve gait, reduce tremors, and maintain quality of life. We help Parkinson's patients stay active, confident, and engaged — because slowing down isn't inevitable.

💪

Multiple Sclerosis Management

Targeted therapy to manage fatigue, spasticity, and balance issues. Our MS patients learn to work with their bodies through flare-ups and remissions, maintaining maximum function through every phase.

⚡

Guillain-Barré Syndrome

Progressive strengthening from ICU to independence. GBS recovery demands patience and precision — we rebuild strength systematically, celebrating every small victory from first toe wiggle to walking unaided.

🌀

Vestibular Rehabilitation

Specialized therapy for chronic dizziness, vertigo, and balance disorders. Using advanced vestibular assessment, we retrain your brain's balance system so you can move through the world with confidence again.

🛡️

Traumatic Brain Injury Recovery

Comprehensive rehabilitation after head injuries — from road accidents on Pune-Mumbai Expressway to workplace falls. We address cognitive, physical, and emotional recovery as one integrated program.

Our Approach

We Don't Just Treat Symptoms.
We Rebuild Capabilities.

Every session at ActiveMind is grounded in neuroplasticity — your brain's remarkable ability to form new connections and rewire itself. We harness that potential with a structured, evidence-based process.

1

Deep Assessment

We spend 90 minutes understanding not just your condition — but your life. Your morning chai routine, the steps to your flat, the grandchild you want to pick up. Real goals, not clinical benchmarks.

2

Custom Protocol

Your brain, your body, your goals — your protocol. We blend Bobath, PNF, motor learning, and task-specific training into a plan that evolves weekly as you progress.

3

Intensive Rewiring

High-repetition, goal-directed therapy that exploits neuroplasticity windows. Every exercise has a purpose — from reaching for a cup to climbing stairs at your Kothrud apartment.

4

Life Integration

Recovery doesn't end at our door. We train your family, adapt your home, and build a maintenance routine that keeps progress going long after discharge. Independence is the real outcome.

A Real Journey

Ramesh's Story: From Hospital Bed
to Morning Walks in Kothrud

Ramesh Kulkarni, 62, had a major stroke while watching cricket at home in Warje. His right side was completely paralyzed. His wife Sunita didn't know if he'd ever speak again. This is their 16-week journey with us.

Week 1–2: The Beginning

First Assessment & Hope

Ramesh arrived in a wheelchair, unable to lift his right arm. After our detailed assessment, Dr. Meera outlined a clear roadmap. For the first time, Sunita said, "I felt like someone had a plan."

Week 3–5: Small Victories

First Movements Return

Through intensive mat exercises and electrical stimulation, Ramesh began flexing his right wrist. He squeezed Sunita's hand — the whole therapy room cheered. These "small" moments are everything.

Week 6–9: Building Strength

Standing & First Steps

With parallel bar support and our therapist Priya by his side, Ramesh took his first steps. Shaky, determined, beautiful. His son recorded a video and the entire family WhatsApp group celebrated.

Week 10–12: Real-World Skills

Daily Tasks Mastered

Buttoning a shirt. Holding a cup of chai. Signing his name. We practice real life, not just exercises. Ramesh started feeding himself independently — something he'd been too embarrassed to admit he missed.

Week 13–16: Independence

Walking in Kothrud Again

Ramesh now walks 500 meters with a cane around Mhatoba Mandir in Kothrud every morning. He plays cards with friends at the local club. Sunita says, "He's not the same Ramesh — he's stronger." We couldn't agree more.
